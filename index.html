Learn to Compost at Home

Source: www.epa.gov

Compost is organic material that can be added to soil to help plants grow. Food scraps and yard waste together currently make up about 30 percent of what we throw away, and should be composted instead. Making compost keeps these materials out of landfills where they take up space and release methane, a potent greenhouse gas.

Reusing through donating

Source: www.epa.gov

Ever heard the saying “One person's trash is another person's treasure”? With reusing this couldn’t be more true. Donate or sell your clothes, tools or other unneeded household goods. Not only will you be helping the environment but you will be helping others.

Benefits of Recycling

Source: www.epa.gov

    1. Reduces the amount of waste sent to landfills and incinerators

    2. Conserves natural resources such as timber, water and minerals

    3. Increases economic security by tapping a domestic source of materials

    4. 1.57 jobs, $76,000 in wages, and $14,101 in tax revenues were generated for every 1,000 tons of materials recycled (according to the Recycling Economic Information Study in 2016).
